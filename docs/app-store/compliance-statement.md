Dear App Review team,

Thank you for your question regarding gambling functionality. We set out below
why Tiki Acca does not require a gambling licence and how the app complies
with Guideline 5.3.

**1. Tiki Acca provides no facilities for gambling.**
The app is a social score-keeping service. Users in a private group each
choose one selection of a shared football accumulator; the app records those
selections, displays bookmaker odds, and keeps points and leaderboards. At no
point does the app accept, negotiate, process, or settle any bet, stake, or
payment, and it contains no in-app purchases or virtual currency. Points are
for social competition only and have no monetary value and no redemption
mechanism.

**2. Regulatory position (United Kingdom).**
Under the Gambling Act 2005, a licence from the Gambling Commission is
required for "providing facilities for gambling" (s.33). Tiki Acca does not
provide such facilities: it does not offer odds for acceptance, hold customer
funds, or participate in any betting transaction. The display of publicly
available odds and hyperlinks to licensed operators is an
advertising/affiliate activity, not a licensable gambling activity. Where a
user chooses to place a bet, they do so directly with an independent
bookmaker licensed by the Gambling Commission, on that operator's website or
app, under that operator's own licence, terms, and player protections.

**3. Distribution and audience controls.**
- App Store availability is limited to the **United Kingdom**, the
  jurisdiction in which all linked operators are licensed.
- The app's age rating is **18+**, and the app independently enforces a
  minimum age of 18 at registration via server-side date-of-birth validation.
- Responsible-gambling signposting (BeGambleAware) is presented in the app.

**4. Data rights.**
Odds and betslip links are licensed via our subscription to The Odds API
[the-odds-api.com]. Fixtures and results are supplied by football-data.org
under our access agreement with that service.
